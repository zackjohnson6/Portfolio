A Warming World

The theory of a warming planet and changes in the Earth’s climate has been around since the early 1800s, at the height of the industrial revolution. In 1824, Joseph Fourier suggested that the atmosphere has an insulating property, causing the Earth to be warmer than it should be compared to its distance from the Sun (2021). In 1856, Eunice Foote discovered that carbon dioxide (CO2) trapped by the Earth’s atmosphere produces heat (2021). And in 1896, Svante Arrhenius predicted, for the first time in human history, that “changes in atmospheric carbon dioxide levels could substantially alter the surface temperature through the greenhouse effect,” (2021).

These pioneers in climate science have helped us to understand that in today’s world the Earth is getting warmer, and the Earth’s climate is being fundamentally changed due to mankind’s release of CO2 and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Here you will find resources related to the causes of climate change, it’s impact on the environment and humans, and what we can do to help mitigate its effects and hopefully put an end to it.
